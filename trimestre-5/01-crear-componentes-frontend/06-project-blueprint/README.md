## Objective

Apprentices will design a visual "Tech Roadmap" (using Canva, PowerPoint, or a basic HTML mockup) to present their short-term, medium-term, and long-term professional plans.

The goal is to accurately demonstrate the differences between the three future structures in English:

-   Present Continuous (fixed arrangements)
-   Be Going To (plans and intentions)
-   Will (probabilities and predictions)

using vocabulary specific to software development.

Inspiracion: Para este punto, siento que puedo basarme en la estructura visual de roadmap.sh (https://roadmap.sh/).

## Overview

As a software developer, planning your sprint, your learning path, and your career milestones (hitos) is essential.

You will create a 3-part visual roadmap outlining your future using English correctly.

---

## The Structure: The 3-3-3 Rule

Your roadmap must include exactly 9 sentences, divided into three specific categories:

### PART 1: The Immediate Sprint
(Present Continuous for fixed arrangements)

Describe 3 things you are doing in the next few days. These are fixed appointments or tasks with a specific time/date (e.g., meetings, exams, project deadlines).

Example: "This Friday, I am presenting the database architecture to my instructor."

---

### PART 2: The Next Milestones
(Be going to for plans and intentions)

Describe 3 things you are going to do later in your training or graduation. These are solid decisions you have made, but the exact date isn't locked in yet.

Example: "Next trimester, we are going to learn Python and backend frameworks."

---

### PART 3: Tech Predictions & Possibilities
(Will for probabilities)

Describe 3 things you will probably do or that will happen in the tech world in the distant future. Use words like probably, maybe, or I think.

Example: "In five years, I will probably work as a remote DevOps engineer."

Aqui esta mi entrega completa con las 9 oraciones requeridas, distribuidas en las 3 partes:

---

### PART 1: The Immediate Sprint

1.  This Thursday, I am presenting the bug-free backend of the 'Novedades' module to my instructor. I am showing the fixes for the mistakes I had last time to ensure everything runs smoothly.

2.  This Sunday, I am refactoring the design of the index.html file in my ADSO repository, where I take notes on the technologist program. During this session, I am updating the SEO attributes, changing the color palette, and reviewing the existing sections.

3.  This sunday night, I am documenting and view the firsts topics that we was seeing in adso. It is about busssnes ruthless, DFD, updating information and use case diagram and other things around UML topics.

---

### PART 2: The Next Milestones

1.  Next month, we are going to review the business rules applied to the backend, frontend, and the core logic of our software development. For instance, in our project modules like 'Horarios' or 'Permisos', we have time constraints; a user cannot request permission for a past date or a year in advance. Things like that show the importance of reviewing business rules.

2.  Next trimester, we are going to review the requirements of our program. Many things change once we start coding, so we are going to check if our development takes into account all original specifications, or if we need to modify some requirements and modules.

3.  Next trimester, we are going to learn more about software security, which is a critical step before deploying a program. We are going to study how it relates to forms, database connections, and secure data exchange. It is going to be really interesting!

---

### PART 3: Tech Predictions & Possibilities

1.  In three years, I will probably finish my Systems Engineering degree. For now, I have not decided which university I will choose, but I have three options: Areandina, Politecnico, or UNAD. I think I will study online because it is more probable that I won't choose an on-campus modality. I want to have more time for a job and focus on learning in front of my computer.

2.  In five years, I will probably work as a remote Full-Stack Developer. I would love to have a remote job. It will be great because I won't have to cross the city to go to work or spend time on public transportation. Currently, I am learning the tech stack and the skills needed to achieve this goal.

3.  In five years, maybe AI will be the most common tool in everyday life and in every job. Everybody will have to work with AI or live with smart devices, like Meta glasses or the Internet of Things (IoT). I think we will probably see robots working like humans.

## Visual Delivery

Create a 1-page visual roadmap. You can use:

-   Canva or PowerPoint (diagram/slides).
-   A basic frontend mockup (HTML/CSS) if you want to practice your coding skills (this is my chosen path).

Tip: Use icons or logos representing the technologies you mention (e.g., Git, SQL, AWS, Java, UML).

---

## The Pitch (Presentation)

Be prepared to present your roadmap to the class in a 1-minute elevator pitch.

Key Pronunciation Tip:
Pay close attention to pronunciation: do not drop the verb "to be" in Parts 1 and 2.

-   Correct: "I am working"
-   Incorrect: "I working"
